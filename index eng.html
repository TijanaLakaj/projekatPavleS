PROJECT PAVLE S

The PavleS project represents an innovative initiative by TeamCAD, realized in partnership with the Technical School Pavle Savić from Novi Sad, creating smart buildings that optimize resources and provide useful information. This revolutionary project sets new standards in efficiency, comfort, and sustainability in both the education and business sectors.

The main goal of the project is to demonstrate the concept of a smart building, characterized by the synergy between Building Information Modeling (BIM) data and various sensors. This approach enables the creation of intelligent objects that provide a wealth of useful information, combining data obtained from sensors with BIM data.

Room maintenance

Find out which rooms are most frequently used

Module designed for analyzing room usage to optimize resources needed for cleaning. It relies on information from 3D BIM models and presence sensors. Based on the data obtained, it is possible to see which rooms have been used and how much, their surfaces, and the total surface area of the rooms used.

This way, cleaning can be planned only for the rooms that have been used.

Temperature and air humidity

Discover which rooms offer the most pleasant stay

Modules designed to process information from sensors measuring temperature and air humidity in the room. This allows for precise air conditioning management and maintenance of optimal conditions for comfort and efficiency.

Additionally, the sensors can detect extreme changes in temperature and humidity, indicating potential issues in the heating or cooling system, as well as unhealthy conditions for occupants.

Room occupancy

Efficiently manage rooms with presence sensors

The room occupancy sensor module enables efficient optimization of room cleaning processes. It provides real-time information on room occupancy, allowing for cleaning schedules to be adjusted according to actual room usage. This avoids unnecessary cleaning of unused rooms, resulting in time and resource savings and reduced maintenance costs.

Additionally, information on occupancy in the last 10 minutes allows for dynamic cleaning planning, contributing to efficiency and optimal use of resources for maintaining space hygiene.

Custom room information

Gain detailed insights into each room's condition

This functionality provides easy access to detailed information about room floors directly from the 3D model itself. When selecting desired rooms, the application provides information about the type of flooring, installation date, and maintenance history, including the last sanding of parquet or floor replacement. This allows you to quickly and easily access all the necessary floor information, facilitating maintenance planning and room management.

With this functionality, floor maintenance can be precisely planned, and timely responses to renovation or repair needs can be made, resulting in extended floor covering life and reduced maintenance costs.

About school

The Technical School "Pavle Savić" in Novi Sad was founded on July 25, 1961, as a Chemical-Technological School. Starting with just two departments, it began its work in the building of the United Milling Industry. Today, the school educates students in three areas: chemistry, non-metals and graphics; personal services; agriculture, food production, and processing, with 17 educational profiles and 1199 students. About Pavle Savic

Pavle Savić (Thessaloniki, January 10, 1909 - Belgrade, May 30, 1994) was a Serbian physicist and chemist, known for his collaboration with Irène Joliot-Curie in Paris, where they discovered isotopes of known elements by bombarding uranium atoms with slow neutrons. He was also the director of the construction of the Vinča Nuclear Institute, and later the founder of the Vinča Institute and its director. Link to wiki page
